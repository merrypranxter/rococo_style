# STYLE_SPEC.md

Canonical visual specification for generating Rococo imagery.
Use as a production spec, prompt scaffold, and QA rubric.

---

## 0) Visual DNA (must keep)

- Exuberant ornament: shell-and-scroll (rocaille) patterns, floral festoons, stucco reliefs covering surfaces.
- Curved asymmetry: C- and S-shaped volutes, counter-curves, compositions that feel spontaneous rather than rigidly mirrored.
- Lightness and elegance: slender forms, delicate curves, avoidance of heavy blocky masses.
- Pastel palette with gold: soft pinks, powder blues, mint greens, creams, pearly greys — paired with gilded accents.
- Theatricality and intimacy: interiors and paintings as stage sets, integrating architecture, painting, sculpture, mirrors, and furniture.

---

## 1) Visual Pillars

### 1.1 Line
- Prioritize C- and S-curves, scrolls, and undulating outlines arranged in intertwined asymmetrical patterns.
- Line hierarchy:
  - **Primary contour**: silhouette and major drapery arcs.
  - **Secondary line**: hair strands, botanical veins, scroll terminations.
  - **Tertiary micro-line**: dense rocaille hatchwork in ornament bands only.
- Curves should feel continuous and fluid; abrupt angular breaks are rare and intentional (only in architectural frames).
- Avoid long straight lines in the ornament field; reserve them for structural panel edges and floor tiles.

### 1.2 Asymmetry
- Even when the overall frame is rectangular, interior ornament and figure placement must avoid strict bilateral mirroring.
- Rocaille motifs at corners should be mirrored with deliberate organic drift — not copy-pasted.
- At least one major diagonal or sweep should cross the composition.

### 1.3 Ornament
- Ornament is structural, not decorative noise: it should lock into frame architecture, figure silhouette, and panel geometry.
- Repetition with variation: recurring scroll and shell motifs with slight scale and orientation shifts.
- Ornament zones (frames, furniture edges, cornice bands) have high curve density; picture fields (sky, skin, fabric) have lower density.

### 1.4 Palette
- Preferred families: powder blue, blush rose, celadon mint, cream pearl, pale yellow, ivory white, warm off-white.
- Metallic accents: warm gold leaf or gilded bronze — used selectively on frames, mounts, and ornament highlights.
- Keep saturation moderate to low; avoid modern saturated neons and deep jewel tones (those read as Baroque, not Rococo).
- Limit total key hues to 4–6 per scene for coherence.

### 1.5 Light
- Light tonal key with gentle modeling; no heavy chiaroscuro.
- Diffuse, soft light suggesting overcast garden or filtered salon illumination.
- Selective specular highlights only on gilding, jewels, and satin fold highlights.
- Shadow edges soft; avoid hard cast shadows.

### 1.6 Texture and Surface
- Surfaces should evoke carved stucco, painted wood, silk, satin, porcelain, and gold leaf.
- Avoid hyper-detailed skin pores, lens noise, or photorealistic 3D material rendering.
- Texture density should never overpower curve and ornament clarity.

### 1.7 Figure Treatment
- Small head and extremities relative to voluminous dress; elegant, elongated proportions.
- Poses: seated, reclining, or gently swaying — avoid stiff military or heroic stances.
- Gestures encode flirtation and conversation: hands reaching, turning, offering, clasping.
- Hair: powdered or elaborately styled, sometimes with feathers or ribbons as compositional extensions.
- Dress: panniers (wide hip extensions), lace, ribbons, and satin — costume should echo and amplify the palette.

### 1.8 Framing and Architecture
- Frames are interdependent with image content: gilded boiserie panels, overdoors, mirror surrounds, and cartouches define image windows.
- Ceiling and overmantel compositions may break the frame with trompe-l'œil effects.
- Mirror presence multiplies space and light — treat mirrors as major compositional actors.

---

## 2) Hard Constraints

- **Must avoid**: heavy Baroque chiaroscuro, monumental religious or dynastic subject matter, straight-leg Neoclassical furniture, modern flat UI aesthetic, harsh photorealistic rendering.
- **Must avoid**: generic "pastel aesthetic" without rocaille, shell/scroll motifs, and Rococo-specific iconography (putti, fête galante figures, gilded boiserie).
- **Must include**: at least one rocaille or scroll-shell indicator plus either a Rococo motif (putti, garland, gilded mirror) or a Rococo subject (fête galante, boudoir portrait, ornamental interior).

---

## 3) Distinction Rules

### 3.1 Rococo vs Baroque
- Baroque: grand scale, heavy masses, strong chiaroscuro, religious/dynastic subjects, more symmetrical architectural discipline, dark jewel palettes.
- Rococo: smaller scale, playful mood, pastels, intimate secular/erotic subjects, asymmetrical ornament, shell-scroll vocabulary.

### 3.2 Rococo vs Neoclassicism
- Neoclassicism: straight lines, clear geometric volumes, restrained ornament, Greco-Roman references, straight furniture legs.
- Rococo: pervasive curvilinear forms, organic ornaments, cabriole legs, sentimental/erotic narratives.

### 3.3 Rococo vs Generic Pastel Aesthetic
- If there is no rocaille/cabriole indicator AND no floral/putti/mirror motifs with gilding, it is not Rococo.
- Generic pastels lack asymmetric scroll ornament, historical garments, and architectural modules.

## 4) Composition Templates

### 4.1 Fête Galante Park Template
- Setting: open parkland with trees, statues, fountains.
- Figures: 3–10 in pastel silk gowns and suits, asymmetrically grouped.
- Required motifs: garland_floral, statue_classical, tree_feathery.
- Optional: lute/violin, mask, fountain, dog.
- Camera: medium-wide, eye-level.
- Light: soft afternoon diffuse.

### 4.2 Boudoir Portrait Template
- Subject: aristocratic woman seated on gilded chaise, three-quarter view.
- Surroundings: boiserie wall panels, ornate gilded mirror, silk draperies.
- Required motifs: rocaille_shell_crest, garland_floral, mirror_overmantel.
- Optional: books, porcelain, small dog, writing desk.
- Camera: three-quarter view, medium shot.
- Light: interior diffuse, selective highlights on satin and gilding.

### 4.3 Ceiling Fresco Template
- Orientation: looking upward; oval or irregular opening to sky.
- Required motifs: putti_cluster, cloud_billow, rocaille_corner_swirl, gilded_stucco_frame.
- Optional: allegorical figures, garlands, trompe-l'œil architectural illusionism.
- Camera: wide-angle, slight perspective distortion.
- Light: bright, radiant, sky-lit.

### 4.4 Rococo Salon Interior Template
- Space: rectangular salon with high boiserie-paneled walls, parquet floor, chandelier.
- Required motifs: boiserie_panel_white_gold, mirror_wall_pair, chandelier_crystal, console_table_gilded.
- Optional: fireplace with overmantel, porcelain vases, tapestries.
- Camera: perspective interior view, moderate wide-angle.
- Light: soft natural from tall windows, supplemented by candlelight sparkle.

## 5) Rococo Fidelity Checklist (Pre-Acceptance Scoring)

Score each item 0, 1, or 2:
- **0** = missing/incorrect
- **1** = partially present
- **2** = strongly present

Maximum score: **40** (20 items). Suggested acceptance threshold: **32+**, with no critical failures.

### 5.1 Curve and Ornament
1. C- and S-curves dominate the ornament field; no long straight lines in ornamental zones.
2. Rocaille or shell-scroll motifs present and structurally integrated.
3. Asymmetry present in motif placement, composition, or figure arrangement.
4. Ornament density is appropriate: high in frame/edge zones, lower in picture fields.

### 5.2 Palette and Light
5. Palette is soft pastel with gold or ivory accents; no heavy jewel tones dominating.
6. Light is diffuse and gentle; no cinematic high-contrast chiaroscuro.
7. Specular highlights are selective (gilding, jewels, satin) not broadly metallic.
8. Overall tonal key is light-to-mid, not predominantly dark.

### 5.3 Motif Fidelity
9. Floral motifs (roses, peonies, garlands) present and stylistically coherent.
10. Putti, cherubs, or mythological figures (if present) match Rococo playful-sweet register.
11. Architectural elements (boiserie, mirrors, stucco) show Rococo vocabulary, not Baroque or Neoclassical.
12. Furniture (if present) shows cabriole legs, bombé shapes, or gilded carved ornament.

### 5.4 Figure and Costume
13. Figure proportions evoke Rococo elegance: small head, voluminous skirt or coat, delicate extremities.
14. Costume includes at least one period marker: panniers, lace, silk ribbons, powdered wig, or tricorn hat.
15. Figure gestures convey flirtation, leisure, or conversation — not heroic action.
16. Hair and accessories extend the compositional rhythm.

### 5.5 Subject and Mood
17. Subject is secular, intimate, and playful — fête galante, portrait, boudoir, or salon scene.
18. Mood reads as elegant, playful, or lightly erotic — not solemn, religious, or austere.
19. Setting reinforces Rococo world: park, salon, boudoir, garden pavilion, or ornamental interior.
20. Overall piece reads as Rococo first, not generic vintage or fantasy.

### Critical Failure Flags (auto-reject)
- Heavy chiaroscuro or dark Baroque palette dominates.
- No rocaille/scroll motifs AND no Rococo subject matter.
- Straight-leg Neoclassical furniture or strict classical architectural orders.
- Photorealistic rendering pipeline that erases ornament language.
- Modern visual language (flat design, neon, sci-fi elements) without intentional stylized bridge.

---

## 6) Practical Usage Notes
- **For generation**: use Pillars + Motif Dictionary + one Composition Template + Fidelity Checklist.
- **For review**: score with Checklist before acceptance.
- **For iterative correction**: target the lowest-scoring sections first (usually asymmetry, palette restraint, and rocaille integration).
- **For shaders**: use curve density maps, ornament band masks, and pastel-gold material presets derived from this spec.
